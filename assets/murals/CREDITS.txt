Minhocário — wall murals
========================

The garage wall carries one of these works per farm, chosen from the farm's own
seed (js/render/murals.js). Every one is in the PUBLIC DOMAIN worldwide.

This file SHIPS with the game, in the same spirit as css/IBMPlexSans-OFL.txt.
Public-domain works carry no legal attribution requirement; recording provenance
anyway is how the next maintainer can verify the licence claim without redoing
the research, and how a work that turns out NOT to be free gets caught.


The public-domain test applied here
-----------------------------------

Every artist below died MORE THAN 70 YEARS AGO. That clears the life+70 term used
by Brazil (Lei 9.610/98) and the EU, and every work also predates 1930, which
clears the United States. The reproductions come from Wikimedia Commons, where a
faithful photograph of a two-dimensional public-domain work carries no new
copyright (Bridgeman v. Corel).

Deliberately EXCLUDED despite being the obvious Brazilian choices: Tarsila do
Amaral (d. 1973) and Anita Malfatti (d. 1964). Neither enters the public domain
until 2044 and 2035 respectively.

What was done to them
---------------------

None of these files is the source image. Each was reduced to a single tonal
channel and normalised onto one shared contrast budget, so that no mural reads as
a different KIND of thing from the others, and so the wall never becomes a second
saturated object competing with the bin. The exact preparation is recorded in
DESIGN.md under "Wall murals"; it is a one-time dev step with no runtime
dependency, exactly like the webfont subsetting.
